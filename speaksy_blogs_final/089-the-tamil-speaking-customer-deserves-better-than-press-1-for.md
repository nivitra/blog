# The Tamil-Speaking Customer Deserves Better Than 'Press 1 for English'

## Let Me Tell You a Story

Let me tell you about Rajesh. He runs an insurance agency in Coimbatore. His customers speak Tamil at home, English at the office, and a fluid mix of both when discussing premiums and policy details.

Rajesh tried one of those 'multilingual' voice platforms. The demo looked great. But when his customer said 'Enaku ₹5 lakh coverage venum, but EMI ethana varum?' — the system froze.

Why? Because it was trained on textbook Tamil and textbook English. Not the Tamil-English that 80 million Indians actually speak every day.

Code-switching isn't a bug in Indian speech. It's the feature. And most voice platforms treat it like an afterthought.

## The Problem Nobody Talks About

Picture this: A customer calls your business. They speak a mix of Hindi and English — what we all call Hinglish. Your IVR says 'Press 1 for English, Press 2 for Hindi.'

The customer presses 1. Gets an English menu. Struggles. Presses 2. Gets Hindi. Still not quite right. They hang up.

That customer just wanted to talk like a normal person.

## How Language Understanding Actually Works

Most people think voice AI 'speaks' languages. That's only half true. The real magic is in **understanding** — and understanding Indian speech is genuinely hard.

Here's why:

**Indian speech isn't 'pure' language.** A customer might say: 'Mujhe ₹5 lakh ka loan chahiye, EMI kitna hoga 3 years ke liye?' That's Hindi + English in one sentence. A 'multilingual' system that switches languages sentence-by-sentence can't handle this. It needs to understand both simultaneously.

**Regional accents vary massively.** Chennai Tamil isn't Madurai Tamil. Mumbai Hindi isn't Lucknow Hindi. A system trained on one accent fails on the other.

**Speakers don't follow scripts.** Your customer might interrupt, change topics, ask unexpected questions, or go silent while thinking. The AI needs to handle all of this naturally.

**What Speaksy does differently:** It's trained on real Indian phone conversations — not textbook language, not translated scripts. It understands Hinglish, Tanglish, Tenglish, Kanglish, Marathi-English, and Indian English as they are actually spoken. With code-switching handled naturally, not as an afterthought.

The result? Your customer feels like they're talking to someone who gets them. Not a robot reading from a translated sheet.

# Why Your Admission Funnel Leaks at the 'Someone Will Call You' Stage

## Let Me Tell You a Story

Rohit's EdTech company prepares for JEE coaching admissions every January. They know the pattern: January to March, inquiry volume goes 10x. April to December, it drops to a trickle.

Last year, Rohit hired 45 telecallers in January. Trained them for 2 weeks. By March, they were handling 3,000 calls a day. By June, 40 of them had nothing to do. By August, Rohit laid off 35.

The cost? ₹42 lakh in hiring, training, salaries, and severance. The feedback from parents? 'I called three times, nobody picked up. I enrolled my child at the institute that called me back.'

Rohit's problem isn't that he needs more callers. It's that he needs a calling system that scales up in January and scales down in April — without the hiring cycle.

## The Problem Nobody Talks About

June to August: EdTech companies hire 50 telecallers for admissions season. September: 40 of them are let go. October to May: The remaining 10 handle 'general enquiries' with zero enthusiasm.

This cycle repeats every year. The cost of hiring, training, and firing? About ₹30-40 lakh per cycle. The result? Inconsistent follow-up and burnt-out employees.

## How Smart Admissions Calling Works

The old way: Hire 45 people in January. Train them for 2 weeks. Hope they remember the fee structure. Watch them make 3,000 calls a day. Fire 35 of them in June. Repeat next year.

The new way:

**Step 1 — Parent fills enquiry form.** Website, Facebook ad, WhatsApp — wherever they find you.

**Step 2 — AI calls within 2 minutes.** In their language. Not a robocall — a conversation. 'Namaste Sharma ji, aapne JEE coaching ke liye enquiry ki thi. Aapke bacche ka 12th ka board kaunsa hai? PCM marks kya hain?'

**Step 3 — Automatic qualification.** The AI captures: board, marks, target exam, preferred batch timing, location, budget range. All stored in your CRM.

**Step 4 — Hot leads flagged.** High-scoring students, early enquiries, repeat callers — marked 'Priority' for your admission counselor.

**Step 5 — Nurturing at scale.** Medium-intent parents get follow-up calls with relevant info — 'Sharma ji, aapke liye scholarship test ka detail bata raha hoon.' Low-intent? Entered into a long-term nurture sequence.

**Step 6 — Scales automatically.** 100 calls or 10,000 calls — same system. No hiring. No firing. No training cycles.

**Your counselors spend time convincing qualified families to enroll.** Not explaining fee structures for the 400th time.

## What This Actually Means for Your Business

**What does a scalable calling system mean for admissions?**

It means you handle 3,000 calls a day in peak season without hiring 45 people.

It means every parent who inquires gets a callback within 2 minutes — not 2 days.

It means your admission counselors spend time on high-intent families who need persuasion and reassurance — not on repeating fee structure details for the 400th time.

And in the off-season? The system doesn't sit idle, eating salaries. It scales down naturally, still handling enquiries, still capturing leads, still nurturing prospects — without the overhead of a room full of bored telecallers.

**You didn't get into education to manage headcount. You got into it to enroll students.**

Old way: 35 telecallers. ₹8 lakh in salaries for 3 months. 40% leads never got called.

New way: AI voice agent + 8 admission counselors.

Results:

That's the thing about speed. It doesn't just convert leads. It builds reputation.

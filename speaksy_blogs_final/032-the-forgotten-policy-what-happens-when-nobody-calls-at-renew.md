# The Forgotten Policy: What Happens When Nobody Calls at Renewal Time

## Let Me Tell You a Story

Suresh has been paying his health insurance premium for 4 years. Never made a claim. Good customer, right?

His renewal came due in March. He was busy with his daughter's wedding. Got one SMS reminder. Ignored it. The policy lapsed.

In June, Suresh had a health emergency. ₹3.2 lakh hospital bill. No active policy.

Whose fault is this? Suresh could have been more careful. But here's the thing: his insurer had his phone number. His email. His address. They had 4 years of premium payment history showing he was a reliable customer.

One phone call in February — a real conversation, not a robocall — would have kept Suresh covered. That call never happened.

## The Problem Nobody Talks About

Your policyholder has been paying premiums for 3 years. Their renewal is due next week. Nobody calls them. They get one SMS. Maybe an email that goes to spam.

They forget. Or they find a better quote. Or they simply don't get around to it.

That's not lapsation. That's a relationship that died from neglect.

## How Proactive Policy Management Works

Insurance isn't a one-time sale. It's a relationship. And like any relationship, it dies without communication.

**Here's how modern voice engagement works for insurance:**

**Renewal calls:** 30 days before expiry, the AI calls. Not an SMS. Not an email. A real conversation. 'Kumar ji, aapka motor policy 7 din mein expire ho raha hai. Premium ₹18,500 — same as last year. NCB bhi protected hai. Renew karun?' Payment via UPI link sent during the call.

**Claim support:** When a claim is filed, the AI becomes the customer's guide. Proactive updates at every step. 'Sharma ji, aapka claim approved ho gaya. ₹1,66,500 aapke account mein 24 ghante mein aa jayega.' No more 'Mera claim ka kya hua?' calls.

**Cross-selling that isn't annoying:** The AI detects life events. 'Kumar ji, records show aapne Whitefield mein property purchase ki hai. Home insurance ₹4,200 mein add kar sakte hain. Bundle pe 15% discount.'

**FNOL (First Notice of Loss):** Car accident? The AI handles the entire claim intimation in 60 seconds. Policy verified. Surveyor assigned. Reference number given. All while the customer is still at the accident spot.

**The common thread:** Every interaction feels personal. Because it is personal — based on the customer's actual policy, history, and needs. Not a generic script.

## What This Actually Means for Your Business

**What does proactive voice engagement mean for insurance?**

It means your reliable customer doesn't lapse because they were busy with their daughter's wedding.

It means every policyholder gets a personal call before renewal — not an SMS that gets ignored, not an email that goes to spam. A real conversation that reminds them why they bought the policy in the first place.

It means when a claim is filed, the customer gets proactive updates at every step — not left wondering for 15 days.

**Insurance is a trust business.** Trust is built through consistent, personal communication. Not through policy documents and premium receipts.

## A Real Example

A general insurance company with 2 lakh motor policies. Renewal rate: 71%. Lapsation cost: ₹4.2 crore annually.

Old renewal process: Bulk SMS (3% response) + email (1.5% response) + human agents calling only high-value policies.

New process: AI voice calls to every policyholder, 30 days before expiry, in their preferred language.

Results after 6 months:

- **Renewal rate: 71% → 84%**
- **Lapsation prevented: ₹1.8 crore in premium retained**
- **Cross-sell: 18% of renewals bundled with home/health insurance**
- **Customer feedback: 'Pehli baar kisi ne call karke yaad dilaya'** ("First time someone called to remind me")

The most unexpected outcome? The AI detected 400+ policies where the vehicle had been sold but premiums were still being paid. Stopped unnecessary payments. Built massive goodwill.

Sometimes the best customer service is stopping them from paying for something they don't need.
